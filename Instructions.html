Survey instructions

Please read carefully: It is very important that you read the instructions carefully.

You will be shown 8 hypothetical scenarios about a potential exercise program. In each scenario, you will be asked to choose between exercise program A or exercise program B. The two exercise programs will differ by location, price, session frequency, level of individualisation, involvement of carers, and chance of making a meaningful recovery. For both scenarios, the program would start after discharge from hospital, be delivered in a group format, and will last 12 weeks. Exercises may include walking, cycling on a stationary bike, using resistance bands, lifting weights, and balance exercises.

Description of exercise program characteristics

Location

This is where the exercise sessions will be delivered. When making your choice, consider how comfortable you are with using technology, the burden of transport, and the amount of support you would need to exercise. The options will be:

  • At home (connected to the exercise instructor via online software).
  • At a leisure centre.
  • Hybrid (a combination of sessions at home and at the leisure centre).

Price

Price is measured in British pounds (£), per month. As the program is 12 weeks long, the overall price will be equal to the monthly price multiplied by three. The options are:

  • Free.
  • £20 per month (£60 for the full 12-week program).
  • £40 per month (£120 for the full 12-week program).

Session frequency

This is how many exercise sessions will take part in per week. The options are:

  • One per week.
  • Three per week.
  • Five per week.

Individualisation

This is how specific the exercises you are given are to your ability and goals. There are two options:

  1. Targeted group exercises with stroke survivors of similar physical ability.
  2. Generic group exercises with stroke survivors of mixed ability.

Carer involvement

This is what the carer receives as part of the program. There are three options.

  1. Carers receive specific education sessions...
  2. Carers also take part in the exercise sessions...
  3. Carers are not involved with the program...

Chance of meaningful improvement in recovery

Different exercise programs may result in different amounts of recovery achieved. The amount of recovery achieved is not guaranteed, but higher intensity rehabilitation is likely to increase the chances of making improvements in things such as walking, thinking ability, strength, etc. A meaningful improvement is something that you would notice in your day-to-day life, for example, walking up the stairs easier, able to use the toilet with more independence. It does not matter what the specific improvement is, as long as it is important and meaningful to you. The options are:

  • 20% chance (2 in 10 people)...
  • 50% chance (5 in 10 people)...
  • 80% chance (8 in 10 people)...
